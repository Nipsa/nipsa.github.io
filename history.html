Our History

The establishment of the Nigeria Society for Prevention of Accident came as a demonstration of passion for safety and the core foundersâ€™ pride as Nigerians. This was because, as at January, 2013, virtually all the current members of the trustees of Nispa were then, members of the Royal Society for Prevention of Accidents Rospa, United Kingdom-, and paying good money in hard currency for the annual membership subscriptions. In what could be called self awakening, we realized in hard way that, the only benefits of our Rospa membership was to use their logo on our control documents as members. There and then, we started having a re-think about the purpose of our membership and how it could benefits the people in our primary domain, Nigeria. For this, our amiable Chairman, the Honorable shiham of NiSPA,Mrs Mano Mbadiwe in collaboration with other stakeholders decided to float a similar institution in Nigeria, basically to carter for accident victims.

That show of dignity, and passion for Safety gave birth to the Nigerian Society for Prevention of Accidents, NiSPA in 2014. And in order to honor a person whose value we share, the NiSPA is hereby institute in Honor of Professor Raymond Omavegbe Agbanobi, from Ovrede, Isoko North Local Government Area in Delta State of Nigeria, and a former Professor in Texas State University, United State of America(USA). The sad news of his demise came from the USA, while his daughter was chairing the meeting where the decision on the subject was being made, in 2013. Instantly, we reasoned that honoring a man of his status, whose core value is rooted on voluntarism evidently fitted into our vision matrix. In addition to voluntary training and advisory services, we have it in mind to establish independent Ambulance Services, the first of its kind in Nigeria. This is to ensure an encompassing value and wider coverage of our activities, especially within the rural corridors, where the miasma of accidents are evident. This calls for more membership, which draws the like minds to provide voluntary services to mankind Memberships is free, but must be rooted on the gift- of-time to carry-out voluntary services.

Aims and Objectives

Fashioned along its British counterpart, the Nigeria Society for Prevention of Accident is registered as incorporated trustees, with the following aims and objectives:

  • To promote Safety in our Society.
  • To organize Seminars, public Lectures and Workshops on topical health and safety issues in our Society.
  • To rehabilitate accident victims in the society.
  • To provide independent Ambulance Services.
  • To compliment the statutory functions of the relevant Agencies and institutions in Nigeria.
  • To encourage business operators to implement safety management systems in their establishments.
  • To encourage people from all works of life to embrace safety culture.
  • To institute Awards to honor men, women and corporate bodies in order to encourage industry best practice rooted on safety.
